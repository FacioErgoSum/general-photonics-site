About General Photonics

General Photonics is at the forefront of developing cutting-edge, open-source functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS) technology. Our mission is to revolutionize the fields of neuroscience and medical imaging by creating accessible, high-quality tools for researchers and clinicians worldwide.

Founded in 2018 by a team of passionate neuroscientists and engineers, we believe in the power of open collaboration to drive innovation in brain imaging technology.

Our Technology

Our flagship product is the Delphi CW-DOT fNIRS Module, an advanced continuous-wave diffuse optical tomography system that offers unprecedented resolution and accuracy in measuring brain activity. Key features include:

  • High-density optode arrays with 256 channels for improved spatial resolution
  • Dual-wavelength light sources (735 nm and 850 nm) for optimal hemoglobin measurement
  • Advanced signal processing algorithms for noise reduction and motion artifact correction
  • Real-time 3D image reconstruction capabilities
  • User-friendly software interface for data analysis and visualization
  • Modular design for easy customization and upgradeability

As an open-source initiative, we believe in the power of collaboration to drive innovation. Our hardware schematics and software are freely available to the scientific community, enabling researchers to adapt and improve upon our technology.

Current Research Applications

Our fNIRS technology is currently being used in a wide range of research areas, including:

  • Cognitive neuroscience studies
  • Brain-computer interface development
  • Neurological disorder diagnosis and monitoring
  • Neurorehabilitation
  • Developmental neuroscience
  • Social cognitive neuroscience

We are constantly exploring new applications and pushing the boundaries of what's possible with fNIRS technology.
